The Untold Fights For Justice.

About eBook

Inspired by the poems and themes of the Harlem Renaissance, Group 4 elected a common theme-no injustice can last forever. This profound truth has been prevalent throughout history and will continue to hold relevance as long as society still exists. Poets Diprajit Nath, Mila Wismar, Noah Steckel, and AJ Reynolds-all with distinct styles-encapsulated this theme. Drawing wisdom from the past to bring light to the future is what will bring us enlightenment.

We hope you enjoy!

Authors

Diprajit

Diprajit Nath

"As a poet, lawyer, musician, and developer, I think wisely
before unleashing my finished poems. I put much effort into
my poem; A Journey for Justice and chose to write about how we humans are all equal in some way and have multiple
similarities and differences that make us who we are. I
combined these strong aspects of my poem with our Group 4
theme; No injustice lasts forever. On the topic of writing and
poetry, A typical aspect I like writing about is life and
existence. I like writing about this because it is a unique aspect most people do not tend to write about, and has/can have deep meaning to it. My inspiration for this poem came from the poem “Common Dust” by Georgia Douglas Johnson. The poem made me think deeply about existence and inspired me to combine that topic with our theme."

Aaron

Aaron Reynolds

"AJ Reynolds is a multifaceted individual with a
passion for poetry, reading, writing, and various
sports. His range of interests adds depth to his
individualistic poetry. He gets motivation and
inspiration from his surroundings, employing skill to
bring out themes that resonate with readers. AJ has a
strong desire to maintain his craft, which is shown in
his meticulous attention to structure, rhyme, and
repetition within the poems he creates. He attributes
most of his creative endeavors to the influences
around him that ignited his artistic journey. Poetry is
his tool to voice his emotions and thoughts, one that
enables him to translate his roots into written word.
AJ’s goal is to make his audience think and feel
deeply with the words they read in every line he 
writes."

Mila

Mila Wismar

"As someone who likes to write, I enjoyed writing this
poem very much. My family has inspired me to write
more and more throughout the years pushing me to
maximize the complexities of my deep emotions. I chose
to write this poem because it felt like the only way i
could really captivate all of these emotions, a feeling
that words could only convey. I decided to not use a
rhythmic structure which allowed me to process the act
of putting things on a page and turning it into a poem.
Now, I can turn to poetry as a means of understanding.
My goal from this poem is to make the audience think
about each line read and ponder about the hidden 
meanings behind it."

Noah 

Noah Steckel

"As a musician, chess player, writer, and
philosopher, I see the world in many different
ways, none of which are more or less true than
others. I owe my variety of perspectives to my
family, who inspired me through the various beliefs
and values they hold. I wrote the poem with a very
existential approach, but still wanted the poem to
be very accessible, so I left a few phrases open to
interpretation (ex. “Conflicting thoughts belong to
a common mind” could refer to the connection to a
god/gods, the sacred nature of spirits, or unity
gained from human nature) to allow a broader
audience, as the topic of the poem demands. I was
inspired primarily by the poems “As the Eagle
Soars” and “The End Poem”. This is my attempt to
recreate their themes in my own style."
